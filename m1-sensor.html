Support Level: L2

Ultrahuman M1 Overview

The Ultrahuman M1 is a wearable glucose monitoring system designed to help users optimize their metabolic health. It works by continuously measuring glucose levels from the interstitial fluid using a CGM (Continuous Glucose Monitor) sensor that is worn on the back of your arm. The data is synced with the Ultrahuman app, where it is analyzed and interpreted.

It is not a medical device (like CGMs used by diabetics), but a consumer-grade tool for wellness tracking.

How It Works

  • The device uses a tiny filament inserted just under the skin (painlessly, for most people).
  • It tracks your glucose levels 24/7, updating every few minutes (bluetooth enabled) or when scanned (NFC enabled sensors).
  • You scan the sensor with your phone (or it auto-syncs, depending on the version) to retrieve the data.

Types Of Sensors & Regions

The Ultrahuman M1 utilizes Abbott's FreeStyle Libre continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) sensors. Specifically, it integrates with the FreeStyle Libre 1, Libre 2, Libre 2 Plus, Libre 3 Plus, Libre Pro sensors and GlucoRx's Vixxa 2 depending on the region and availability.

These sensors are FDA-approved, medical-grade devices that measure glucose levels in the interstitial fluid. Ultrahuman packages these sensors in their original form, including additional accessories like alcohol wipes and protective patches.

  • India: GlucoRx's Vixxa 2, Libre 1, Libre Pro
  • UK/EU: Libre 2 and Libre 2 Plus
  • UAE: Libre 2 and Libre 2 Plus
  • USA: Libre 3 Plus

Ultrahuman App Features

  • Real-time glucose feedback: See how your body responds to food, workouts, sleep, and stress.
  • Glucose score: A simplified number to help interpret the quality of your metabolic response.
  • Actionable insights: Personalized recommendations to improve energy, focus, and metabolic flexibility.
  • Food and lifestyle tracking: Correlate specific meals or habits with glucose spikes or dips.

Why People Use It

  • Fitness optimization: Athletes use it to fine-tune performance and recovery.
  • Fat loss: Helps users avoid glucose spikes that can hinder fat metabolism.
  • Health monitoring: Offers insights, even for non-diabetics.
  • Biohacking: Used by enthusiasts looking to optimize longevity and cognitive performance.

What's In The Box

  • A CGM sensor (Ultrahuman partners with Abbott FreeStyle Libre and GlucoRx's for the hardware)
  • 3 hypoallergenic protective skins for the CGM
  • An alcohol wipe

Sensor Application Tips

The sensor must be applied when you are completely fasted (early morning), and you must also avoid food and drink during the 60-minute warming-up window. This is necessary so that the sensor can accurately calibrate your baseline.

  • Wait for the alcohol wipe to dry completely before applying the sensor. The moisture might prevent the adhesive from sticking to the skin. Waiting 3-4 minutes before applying the sensor after wiping the area is a must.
  • Shave the area of the application completely so that the sensor adhesive can stick to the skin. Hair can cause the sensor to fall off.
  • There should be no tattoos, scars, or stretch marks in the application area, as this can affect the calibration.

Applicator Fail

Users may report that the applicator pin is missing or that the applicator was triggered before placement. In both cases, the sensor cannot be applied after this happens.

What the user might see
  • Missing applicator pin.
  • Applicator triggered before placement.

Ask for photos of the applicator. Add screenshots if available.

Agent diagnosis steps
  • Ask the user to share photos of the applicator.
  • Confirm whether the pin is missing or the applicator was triggered accidentally.
Resolution
  • This cannot be reapplied.
  • Replace the sensor.

Sensor Discomfort

Related Tags: sensor_discomfort_replacement, sensor_discomfort

Overview

This is when a user reaches out saying they are experiencing discomfort due to the sensor. This can include rashes, swelling, bleeding, pain, numbness, or tingling sensations in the arm.

Agent diagnosis steps
  • Ask if the pain/discomfort has increased or reduced since applying the sensor.
  • If the user says the pain is unbearable, advise them to remove the sensor.
  • Ask them to rate the pain on a scale of 1-10.
  • If the pain is 7-10, offer a replacement immediately.
  • For all discomfort cases, subtly ask if they are willing to wait a few hours to see if the pain goes down.
  • Make an entry in the M1 Discomfort Sheet.
Resolution
  • Replace depending on severity (7-10) or if unbearable.
  • Advise removal if pain is severe.
  • Provide calming guidance if mild and user agrees to wait.

Sensor Knock Off

Related Tags: sensor_knock_off

Overview

Knockoffs occur when the sensor gets dislodged from the user’s arm. This can happen due to improper application, impact, or excessive sweating.

What the user might see
  • Sensor detached.
  • Adhesive coming off.
  • Patch lifting.
Agent diagnosis steps
  • The application area must be shaved.
  • They must wait for the alcohol wipe to fully dry before applying the sensor.
  • The black cover patch must be securely adhered to the sensor.
Resolution
  • If the user has UltrahumanX, replace the sensor.
  • If they do not have UltrahumanX, send the Non_UHX_Knockoff macro.

Invalid Readings

Related Tags: sensor_invalid_readings, sensor_invalid_reading_midway

Overview

This refers to cases where the sensor displays incorrect or frozen readings for extended periods. These issues can occur due to rare manufacturing defects or during the initial calibration window.

What the user might see
  • Reading stuck on the same value for a long time.
  • No glucose graph being generated.
  • Readings that don’t match the user’s symptoms or expected trends.
Agent diagnosis steps
  • Ask the user to share a screenshot of the current reading or graph.
  • Check Stints by going to: Internal > CGM Stints.
  • Or check Internal > Lifetime Data.
  • Look for raw values that are critically low (<40) or critically high (>400).
  • Confirm if invalid readings have been continuous for 6+ hours.
  • Check if the sensor is within the first 24 hours, since calibration drift is expected during that window.
Resolution
  • If the readings have been invalid for 6+ hours, replace the sensor.
  • If within the first 24 hours, reassure the user that readings may stabilise once calibration completes.
  • If the user asks why this happens, inform them it is due to rare manufacturing-related defects.

Failed to Start

Related Tags: SENSOR_FAILED_TO_START

Overview

This issue occurs when the sensor does not properly initialize, causing the app to repeatedly show a 60-minute warm-up countdown after each scan.

What the user might see
  • The 60-minute countdown timer appears after every scan.
  • The sensor never progresses beyond warm-up.
  • Continuous loop of “Sensor starting” messages.
Agent diagnosis steps
  • Confirm that the user has scanned the sensor correctly.
  • Ask how many times they’ve seen the 60-minute countdown repeat.
  • Check Sensor States from the dashboard: Internal > Sensor States.
  • If the user has seen the countdown 2-3 times, the sensor has failed to start.
  • This logic applies specifically to Libre 1 in India.
Resolution
  • Replace the sensor once the repeated countdown loop is confirmed.

Expired Prematurely

Related Tags: sensor_expired_prematurely

Overview

This refers to cases where the sensor stops working or shows an expiration message before the intended 14/15 day usage window.

What the user might see
  • Reading stuck on the same value.
  • The glucose graph not updating after scans.
  • The app showing “Your sensor has expired” despite being well before the 14/15 day period.
Agent diagnosis steps
  • Ask the user to share a screenshot of the reading or error.
  • Confirm the sensor activation date to ensure it is still within the valid stint.
  • Validate that the issue is occurring before the intended expiry window.
  • For manual sensors, check Sensor States to review sensor age. If the age drops to 0 midway through the stint, the sensor has expired. If the age remains stuck at the same value despite multiple scans across the day, that also indicates expiry.
Resolution
  • Directly replace the sensor in all such cases.

Baseline Issues

Related Tags: sensor_baseline_replacement, sensor_baseline_error, sensor_baseline

Overview

Users may report that their readings appear consistently too low or too high. If the stints and states look normal, this could be a baseline calibration issue that can be corrected using manual calibration.

What the user and agent might see
  • Readings that seem unusually low or high.
  • Glucose trends that do not match expectations.
  • No visible issues in sensor states or stints.
Agent diagnosis steps
  1. Ask the user if they have compared the reading with a glucometer.
    • If they do not have one: India or UAE: send a glucometer. Other regions: ask them to purchase one and offer reimbursement (discuss with SME).
    • This applies only if the sensor was purchased from Ultrahuman.
    • Verify the purchase before issuing a glucometer.
  2. Ask the user for a fasted glucometer reading and the exact time it was taken.
  3. Go to Lifetime Data and check the Filtered Trend. Compare the CGM value 20 minutes after the glucometer timestamp (or closest available timestamp).
  4. If the difference is more than 15 points:
    • Go to CGM Stints.
    • Search using the user’s email ID.
    • Open the latest stint.
    • Edit and calibrate the sensor accordingly.
  5. If the difference is within 15 points, inform the user that this deviation is normal and the sensor is aligned.
Resolution
  • Perform manual calibration if the deviation is greater than 15 points.
  • No calibration is needed if the sensor is within the first 24 hours.
  • After calibration, if the user wants to compare again, the glucometer reading does not need to be fasted.
  • If accurate readings are still not observed post calibration, take a call and replace the sensor.
Example

If a user reports a fasted glucometer reading of 80 at 9:35 am, check the Filtered Trend for 20 minutes after 9:35 am (or the closest timestamp). If the CGM reading is 40 points higher, adjust the calibration by -40 via CGM Stints > User Email > Edit.

Unable to Scan

Applicable regions: India primarily (some Libre 2 users outside India as well, since NFC is required for the first scan).

Related tags: sensor_scan_not_working, Sensor_scan_not_working_replacement

Overview

This issue occurs when a user is unable to scan the sensor and the phone or app shows no response during scanning. This is commonly related to NFC issues on the phone.

What the user might see
  • No response from the phone or app while scanning the sensor.
  • Sensor not detected despite multiple scan attempts.
Agent diagnosis steps
If on iOS
  • Copy the user's External ID or Email ID.
  • Search for it on Slack, usually in the #M1-Notifications channel.
  • Review the logged errors.
  • Refer to this Slack message for error mapping.
  • If the error is System resource unavailable, it indicates an NFC issue.
  • Ask the user to restart the phone.
If on Android
  • Ask the user to toggle NFC off and on.
  • Ask them to restart the phone.
For both iOS and Android
  • Ensure there is no phone cover interfering with NFC scanning.
  • Ask the user to try scanning again post troubleshooting.
  • Check Sensor States on OMS to ensure there are no hits.
Resolution

If the sensor remains unresponsive after all troubleshooting steps and there are no hits in Sensor States, replace the sensor.

Before issuing a replacement, always run these two checks:

  1. Confirm whether it is an internal sensor.
    • Check via CGM Stints.
    • Can also verify in CGM Sensor Inventories on OMS.
    • Or confirm using the invoice.
  2. Ensure that replacements issued are not more than 50% of the total sensors purchased by the user.
    • Exceptions can be made after consulting with an SME/Lead and after their approval.

Non-Live Sensor Missing Data

Applicable regions: India (specifically applicable to Freestyle Libre 1).

Related tags: sensor_scan_over_8_hours, sensor_how_many_times_to_scan

Overview

Users in India using non-live sensors like Freestyle Libre 1 may reach out saying they are seeing gaps in their glucose data. This typically happens when the sensor has not been scanned within the required time window.

What the user might see
  • Missing data or gaps in the glucose graph.
  • Data available only for the last few hours.
  • No historical readings for earlier hours.
Agent diagnosis steps
  • Check CGM Stints.
  • Check Sensor States.
  • If stints look fine, note that brief invalid readings can sometimes cause small gaps.
  • If gaps persist, check scan timestamps in Sensor States.
  • Inform the user that for non-live sensors, they must scan the sensor at least once every 8 hours.
  • This rule does not apply to live sensors unless they get disconnected.
Examples

A. The sensor has been scanned every 1-2 hours so no data gaps.

B. There is a gap of more than 8 hours.

  • Last scan was at 9:36 pm.
  • Next scan was at 7:02 am.
  • This creates a 10-hour gap.

Because non-live sensors only store data for 8 hours, the user will see data for the last 8 hours but will miss the first 2 hours after the earlier scan.

Resolution
  • Educate the user about scanning the sensor at least once every 8 hours.
  • No replacement is required in such cases.

Libre 1, 2, or Pro in the US

Applicable regions: US.

Overview

This applies to cases where users have purchased sensors in India and are using them in the US. Ultrahuman officially supports only Libre 3 Plus in the US, which cannot be scanned directly using the Ultrahuman app.

What the user might see
  • No Scan button visible in the app.
  • Unable to scan their sensor in the US.
  • Sensor appears unsupported.
Agent diagnosis steps
  • Confirm that the user is currently located in the US.
  • Identify whether the user is using Libre 1, Libre 2, or Libre Pro purchased in India.
  • Explain that the Scan button is hidden because the app assumes the sensor is Libre 3 Plus.
Resolution

Create a manual stint to inform the app that the user is using a non-Libre 3 Plus sensor.

Steps:

  1. Copy the user's email ID.
  2. Go to CGM Stints.
  3. Click New CGM Stint.
  4. Paste the email ID.
  5. Set Sensor Type to Libre 1 / Libre 2 / Libre Pro.
  6. Enable Algo.

Once completed, the user should start seeing the Scan button in the app.

US M1 Sync Issues

Applicable regions: US.

Related tags: Libre3_integrationerror

Overview

In the US, Ultrahuman uses the Abbott Freestyle Libre 3 Plus sensor. The Libre 3 Plus does not connect directly with the Ultrahuman app. Instead, data flows through the Libre 3 Plus app and is then pulled into Ultrahuman via the integration provider Terra.

Data flow: Libre 3 Plus sensor → Libre 3 Plus app → Terra → Ultrahuman app.

Users usually understand the connection flow. Most queries are related to data sync delays or missing data.

What the user might see
  • Delay in glucose data syncing.
  • Missing readings in the Ultrahuman app.
  • Recent data not visible.
Agent diagnosis steps
  • Check if the integration is switched on: Integrations Users.
  • Confirm the Libre 3 Plus app is running in the background at all times.
  • Ensure the phone and sensor are in close proximity.
  • Check if the user's data is visible on the LibreView dashboard. This step is critical.
  • Try a manual sync via Integrations Users.
  • Check CGM Stints on the Ultrahuman dashboard (a 20 minute lag is expected in the US).

  • Users can use externally procured Libre 3 Plus sensors with our app, no Lite subscription needed.
Resolution

  • Do not troubleshoot expired or near-expiry sensors for replacement under this flow.
